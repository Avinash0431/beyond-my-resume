CareerOS

CareerOS

Proof the thinking travels, not another case study

Glide proves this works in travel. CareerOS proves it isn't a fluke. Same chain, a completely different domain: my own job search, automated.

Open CareerOS →
The hard callWhether to let CareerOS auto-apply on my behalf to maximize volume.
RejectedFull automation, applying to every match without review. No user testing caught this. I did, before it shipped.
Trade-off acceptedOne extra manual step, every time, because automation should remove work, not remove my decision.
OutcomeCareerOS automates coordination: sourcing, tailoring, follow-up cadence. It never automates the "yes."

Not Adjectives

What working with me actually looks like.

"Collaborative" and "data-driven" mean nothing until they're a behavior. Here's what they turn into on my team.

Instead of "collaborative"

I involve engineering before requirements become expensive to change.

Instead of "data-driven"

I don't ship a feature until I know which single metric will prove it worked.

Instead of "user-centric"

I ask what happens if we're wrong before I ask what happens if we're right.

Instead of "decisive"

I'll say "I don't know yet" out loud rather than fake a yes or no for comfort.

Instead of "scrappy"

I ship the smallest version that can actually be proven wrong, not the safest one.

Instead of "strategic"

I say no to three good ideas so one gets enough attention to actually work.

The Uncomfortable Part

Three decisions I'd never make again.

Most PMs edit these out. They're the fastest way to see how someone actually thinks, so they stay in.

01. Glide

Compliance ≠ Trust

I built a document checklist to resolve visa anxiety. Users kept asking "but will I actually get approved?" A checklist never answered that.

LessonA checklist satisfies the rule. It doesn't touch the fear behind it.
Today I'd doPrototype the prediction, however rough, before the checklist. Even a bad guess answers the real question better than a perfect form.
02. CareerOS

Volume Isn't a Strategy

I optimized for more applications. 12 applications, 0 replies, in 4 days closed that question fast.

LessonSignal-to-noise beats raw effort, every time.
Today I'd doSet the "reply rate," not "applications sent," as the one metric before writing a line of code.
03. CareerOS

Automating Away Agency

I designed auto-apply on a candidate's behalf. No user testing caught this one. I did, and killed it before it shipped.

LessonAutomation should remove work, not remove the user's decision.
Today I'd doWrite the one-line "who decides?" test into the spec on day one, not catch it in review.
