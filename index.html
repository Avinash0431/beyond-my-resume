Case 001

Glide

Travel super app, version 3 of 3

The QuestionCan travel planning become predictable instead of stressful?

0 of 11 travel platforms I audited would tell you if you'd actually get a visa.

Enter the full case study →
HypothesisA document checklist would resolve visa anxiety.
EvidenceUsers kept asking anyway: "but will I actually get approved?" The checklist never answered that, twice.
DecisionKill the checklist. Rebuild around a unified cart and checkout that bets on trust over paperwork.
Trade-offSlower to build and riskier to be wrong about, but it finally answered the real question.
Outcome40% fewer booking steps. Version three is the one that shipped.

The QuestionCan job search coordination be automated without taking away the candidate's judgment?

The fastest workflow wasn't the one people trusted.

Enter the full case study →
Decision Moment 3 of 5

Quick call, before you read on: auto-apply on the candidate's behalf, or require manual confirmation every time?

HypothesisAuto-applying to every match would maximize volume.
EvidenceNo user testing caught the problem. I did, before it shipped: automation was about to remove the user's choice, not just their work.
DecisionKill full automation. Keep one manual step at the final send, every time.
Trade-offSlower per application, but the candidate stays in control of every "yes."
OutcomeCareerOS automates sourcing, tailoring, and follow-up cadence. It never automates the decision to apply.

Not Adjectives

What working with me actually looks like.

"Collaborative" and "data-driven" mean nothing until they're a behavior. Here's what they turn into on my team.

Collaborative

I involve engineering before requirements become expensive to change.

Data-driven

I don't ship a feature until I know which single metric will prove it worked.

User-centric

I ask what happens if we're wrong before I ask what happens if we're right.

Decisive

I'll say "I don't know yet" out loud rather than fake a yes or no for comfort.

Scrappy

I ship the smallest version that can actually be proven wrong, not the safest one.

Strategic

I say no to three good ideas so one gets enough attention to actually work.
